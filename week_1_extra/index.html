PERSONAL STATEMENT

I’m an enthusiastic software engineer that strives to create high quality and highly maintainable software that meets the requirements of the end user. I strongly believe that the best software is software that people want to use, rather than are forced to use.

I take pride in making the jobs of others easier, in enabling people to focus on the more interesting aspects of their job and in teaching others the lessons I’ve learned.

In the past few years I’ve seen the benefits of having a more structured development process with well-defined procedures to follow, I’ve also helped to document and implement some of these processes. I’ve developed an interest in accelerating the adoption of these processes and helping the technical team understand, not only what the processes / procedures are, but why we have them and why we should be following them. I also strongly believe that we should be using all the available tools to support and reinforce our processes / procedures as this will lead to an increase in productivity and a reduction in the amount of rework that is currently carried out over a project’s lifetime.

I understand that managing people may not always be a ‘fun’ task, but I have observed that guiding team members to a higher level can be very rewarding. Working with our student placements for example and seeing them take the various lessons on board and grow into better engineers with a greater understanding that it’s not just about doing the work but being proud of what they’ve accomplished, has been a pleasure. A pleasure that I can see far outweighing the less gratifying parts of leading a team.

I have a solid working knowledge of the principles behind the sensor technologies currently employed in Apollo’s various ranges of fire detectors. Over the past 22 years I’ve worked with most of the products Apollo manufacture. From 2006 onwards, I’ve helped to handle and resolve field issues, either by debugging hardware or by writing new software tools to help reproduce specific hypothetical scenarios that could potentially trigger the observed / reported symptoms.
